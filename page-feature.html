About The Program

The Cape Fear Community College Pharmacy Technology program trains individuals to assist and support licensed pharmacists in providing healthcare and medications to patients.

Responsibilities for Pharmacy Technicians include maintaining inventories, patient records, and clerical requirements of third party and regulatory agencies. In addition, graduates will perform all aspects involved with the filling of prescriptions and institutional orders including interpreting physician’s orders, compounding non-sterile and sterile formulations, packaging, labeling, and billing.

Graduates have gone on to work in hospitals, nursing homes, private and corporate community pharmacies, research laboratories, wholesale drug companies, and pharmaceutical manufacturing facilities. Starting wages earned by graduates are competitive based on pharmacy practice settings.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment rates for pharmacy technicians is projected to grow 12 percent from 2016-2026.

Donna Williamson, AAS Degree Candidate 2019

Cape Fear Community College offers two pharmacy technician pathways:

One-Year Diploma Program (47/48 credits)

The diploma program is a full time/daytime, and three semester (fall, spring, and summer) program. The program consists of five clinical rotations in the local community and hospital pharmacies. Students will have the opportunity to take the Board Certification in the summer semester (currently, the pass rate from CFCC students is 100%). Some classes include Pharmacy Practice, Calculations, Pharmacology, Trends in Pharmacy and Professional Practice.

Associate in Applied Science Degree Program (74/75 credits)

This curriculum is an extended version of the diploma program. However, the North Carolina Board of Pharmacy recognizes graduates with AAS Degrees in Pharmacy Technology and board certification as Validating Technicians. Validating Technicians will be awarded an expanded role in pharmacy practice in North Carolina and will be allowed to validate certain stocking and unit-dose packaging functions of other pharmacy technicians in the institutional pharmacy setting.
